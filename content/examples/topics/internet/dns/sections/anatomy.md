A domain name is a series of **labels** separated by dots — in `www.example.com`, the labels are `www`, `example`, and `com`. [[cite:rfc1034]]

Read it **right to left**, from least specific to most specific. The rightmost label, `com`, is the **top-level domain (TLD)**. Then `example` is the domain registered under it, and `www` is a host within that domain.

There is one more label you never type: an empty label on the far right, **the root**. The fully written name is `www.example.com.` with a trailing dot. That final dot is the top of the whole tree.

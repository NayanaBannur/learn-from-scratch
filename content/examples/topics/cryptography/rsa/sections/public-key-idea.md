The idea is to split the key in two: a **public key** anyone may see, and a **private key** you never reveal.

Picture an open padlock. Anyone can pick it up and click it shut on a box — that is the public key. Only you hold the metal key that reopens it — the private key.

So anyone can lock a message *to* you, yet only you can unlock it. The two keys are a matched pair, made together, but holding the public one tells you nothing about the private one.

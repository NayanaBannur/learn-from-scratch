Two people want to exchange private messages. To scramble them they need a shared secret key — but they have never met, and every byte they send crosses a wire anyone can record.

Until the 1970s this looked unavoidable: to share a secret, you first had to share a secret. Keys travelled by trusted courier and locked briefcase.

In 1976 Whitfield Diffie and Martin Hellman framed the way out — a scheme where the scrambling key can be announced in public, while the unscrambling key never travels at all. [[cite:dh76]] RSA, in 1978, was the first practical method to deliver it. [[cite:rsa78]]
